The human factor represents the weakest point and greatest risk in an organisation’s cyber defence – this is particularly so in the case of ‘social engineering’ attacks. With increasing digitalisation of processes and communications, hackers and cyber-criminals are frequently able to capitalise on the general lack of IT security awareness among a company’s workforce by creating well-orchestrated hacks designed to gain access to a company’s network via an unsuspecting employee. Social engineering attacks can come in a variety of forms - such as ‘phishing’, ‘spear phishing’, ‘vishing’, ‘pretexting’ and ‘baiting’ – but they all involve an element of deception. Typically, an employee or group of employees will be tricked into revealing critical data such as passwords and bank account details, or an employee will inadvertently open an email attachment which contains malware. The ARX platform has been designed to provide consistant and on-going training support to an organization’s departments and individuals. ARX will use interactive training modules. The specific aims are:

  • To increase employees’ overall awareness of the threat of ‘social engineering’ attacks.
  • To build improved staff behaviour and help all employees to be aware of potential inconsistencies in email traffic etc..
  • To recognise actual hacking attacks while they are in progress and to identify the source of a a breach as quickly as possible.
Training modules will be organised and delivered via the ARX system, and will be tailored to meet the specific needs of departments and business operations.

The ARX platform will monitor supplier compliance and adherence to the lead organization’s policy and agreed protocols. The on-going monitoring identifies unusual patterns and critical events in the supply chain. By identifying out of the ordinary supplier behaviour the platform will enable the lead organization to predict possible weak spots in its overall supply chain. The platform will enable the lead organization to transform raw data into actionable intelligence and help drive early and effective decision making.
